Banking

Banking is arguably the most important service in the modern economy and it's easy to understand why. All of the world's money filters through banks, with business revenues and personal incomes sent to bank accounts while transactions become increasingly cashless. Although paying for banking services is incredibly rare, holding money and allowing you to accrue interest remains a service even without payment. Employees in this industry are responsible for handling transactions, ensuring the security of client accounts and keeping an eye out for potential cases of financial fraud.

Insurance

Insurance is another major industry, primarily focused on assuring that clients don't face financial ruin in the event that they find themselves in accidents or incidents that were not their fault. Where you might believe insurance to be a personal product, it's important to remember that the insurance industry also underwrites ventures such as business projects, film productions and even highly specific cases such as insuring football clubs against their players getting injured. Insurance employees are responsible for calculating the cost of policies, processing payouts and investigating incidents leading to claims.

Property letting

Letting homes is a major industry. Due to increasing prices of properties, individuals are more commonly turning to companies that let properties to tenants and living in rented accommodation rather than buying homes outright. Furthermore, property letting companies work closely with businesses to ensure that they have an office space in which to work. For many businesses buying office space outright is unfeasible, so the majority of companies instead choose to rent office space. Letting employees negotiate prices with their clients, complete paperwork to let properties out and guide prospective tenants through viewings of properties.

Teaching and education

Although teaching and education is an industry most commonly offered by the state, it's heavily based on providing a service. Education may be one of the most used services around, with everyone going through the system at some point whether in the private sector or the state schooling system. Employees in the education sector complete administrative tasks such as marking in addition to teaching students and managing staff to ensure a comprehensive provision of education to all students.

Personal training

Personal training is a service that is increasing in popularity as people become more and more health-conscious. This industry focuses on getting long-term health results for clients, pushing them on to fitness goals and aims that they haven't previously been able to attain. A personal trainer is responsible for motivating their clients to achieve their aims, designing customised diet and exercise plans and tracking fitness outcomes in the long run.

Architecture

Architecture is a key service requiring a wealth of expertise, qualifications and an eye for detail. This industry and the services within it are responsible for designing buildings, calculating the forces that they experience in day-to-day life and even designing the smallest aspects of the interior of a property. The main roles of an architect include staying in contact with clients, designing based on a brief and remaining within a budget.
